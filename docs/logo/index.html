The Puzzle — 8 Steps

Each decision is forced by the previous. Can you derive the logo from scratch?

The apex is at φ³ from the top of the canvas.
φ³ = 4.236, so 1000/φ³ = 236. The apex sits one φ³-th of the canvas from the top edge. Top third: 236 < 333 ✓.

The base endpoints are at φ⁴ from each edge, with 3-4-5 linking them to the apex.
Left base x = 1000/φ⁴ = 146. Right base x = 1000 − 146 = 854. The space below apex (764) is divided by φ: base y = 236 + 764/φ = 708. Height = 472. Half-base = 354. 354 × 4/3 = 472 ✓. Stroke = 590. 590 : 472 : 354 = 5 : 4 : 3.

6
The two gradients form a cross-fade connected by √2.
Left leg transitions at 1/√2 ≈ 70.7% from apex (stays bright longer). Right leg transitions at 1-1/√2 ≈ 29.3% from apex (darkens early). At any height, the two legs have opposite brightness — the light "rotates" from left to right as you descend.
Left: midpoint at 70.7% · Right: midpoint at 29.3%
Cross-fade: brightness shifts from left → right
7
The background matches the shape direction (↘) with contrast set by φ.
Background luminance = (1 − shape_luminance) / φ. Where the shape is brightest (apex), bg is black (0). Where the shape is darkest (base), bg is 1/φ = 61.8% grey. Same direction as shapes — they darken together.
Max bg = 1/φ ≈ 0.618 → #9e9e9e
Background → same ↘ direction as shapes

The Meta-Rule

eiπ + 1 = 0
Euler's Identity does not set any single value. It is the rule that governs how the rules relate — five seemingly unrelated constants forming a single system. The logo mirrors this: five design rules, one unified mark.
e → Dot position (Gap = D/e) i → Gradient cross-fade (left-right rotation) π → Moon crater positions + digits 1 → White at apex 0 → Black at base + negative space
The i (imaginary unit) is the cross-fade between the two gradients — as you descend the A, the light rotates from the left leg to the right leg, just as eiπ rotates through the complex plane.
